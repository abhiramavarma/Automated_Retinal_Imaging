About This Project

This project demonstrates an automated system for the early diagnosis of Diabetic Retinopathy and its potential links to cardiac and ocular diseases using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN).

Purpose

Diabetic Retinopathy (DR) is a common complication of diabetes that affects the eyes, often leading to blindness if not detected early. Recent research also suggests potential correlations between the state of retinal blood vessels and systemic conditions like heart disease and glaucoma. This tool aims to provide an automated analysis of retinal images to assist in identifying the severity of DR and highlighting potential associated risks.

Model

The system utilizes a deep learning model based on the DenseNet121 architecture, pre-trained on the ImageNet dataset and fine-tuned on a dataset of retinal images labeled with different stages of Diabetic Retinopathy (No DR, Mild, Moderate, Severe, Proliferative DR).
